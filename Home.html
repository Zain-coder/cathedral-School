History of Lahore Diocese Board of Education

The Lahore Diocesan Board of Education (LDBE) is a body registered under the Society's Act XXI of 1860. It established its first school, i.e., St. Denys’ School in Murree Hills in the year, 1880. A year later, in 1881 Cathedral School was opened in Lahore, and in 1882 Station School in Rawalpindi Cantonment was started. After this many other schools were established. Over the years the aim of the Lahore Diocesan Board of Education has been to take education to the door steps of people. LDBE is a charitable organization and it runs its educational institutions solely for the purpose of education and not for profit. The Lahore Diocesan Board of Education has its institutions in cities as well as in the small towns.
